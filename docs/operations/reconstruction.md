
# Reconstruction Model

## Purpose
Describe how an investigator interprets Probity records after an incident.

## Investigation Flow
An investigator:

1. Identifies the action of interest
2. Retrieves the corresponding record
3. Examines the decision snapshot
4. Follows lineage to related records
5. Evaluates the decision environment

The process reconstructs context, not system behavior.

## Questions Answered
Probity records help answer:
- What information did the system rely on?
- Who was attributed responsibility?
- What action was committed?
- What result was observed?

## Limitations
Probity does not determine correctness or intent.
Human judgment remains required.

## Reconstruction Questions

A complete record MUST allow a reviewer to determine:

1. Who caused the action
2. What the system believed at the moment of decision
3. What authority permitted the action
4. Why the chosen action was selected
5. What external effect was committed
6. Whether the record was altered after creation
